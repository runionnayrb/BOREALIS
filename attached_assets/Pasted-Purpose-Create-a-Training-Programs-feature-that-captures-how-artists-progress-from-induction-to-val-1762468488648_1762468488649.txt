Purpose

Create a Training Programs feature that captures how artists progress from induction to validation, then to competency for a specific position. This page will be used by rigging, coaching, and stage management to plan, run, and sign off training, with strict permissions and a complete audit trail. It must integrate cleanly with the existing lineup and competency structures, but this scope focuses on Training Programs.

Core outcomes

Every artist has a clear, step by step path for a given act or cue.

Only authorized roles can sign off their department steps.

An artist cannot move forward until prerequisites are complete.

A final stage management validation is required to perform in show conditions.

Once all steps are complete, the system can assign a competency for that position.

Use the team transcript to shape stages

Use the conversation notes to pre build sensible default stages and language. Examples to include as templates:

Induction and orientation, safety procedures, harness fitting

Technical induction, harness on, connect to trolley, go inverted a few times under supervision

Track familiarization, walk the track halfway, return, then full track walk

Full track rehearsal, run both tracks P1 and P2 with supervision
These are examples. The system must allow adding, removing, and reordering stages for any act or cue.

Example training program

Name: Globe Cable
Steps in order:

Cable rigging induction

Cable choreography with coaching

Cable training in work lights

Cable validation in show conditions
Each step is owned by a department. Rigging owns rigging items. Coaching owns choreography or movement. Stage management owns anything non department specific and always performs the final show readiness validation.

Scope of this page

A new section named Training Programs with two views and one modal flow.

View A, Programs list

Filter and search by scene, act, cue, department, status, and tag

Color tags, green for Act, yellow for Cue, orange for Acrobatic Cue

Columns, Program name, Scene, Act or Cue, Departments involved, Artists in progress, Artists validated, Last activity

New Program button

View B, Program detail

Header, Program name, Scene, Act, Cue, Color tag, Departments involved

Tabs

Steps, ordered list of training steps with owner department, prerequisite flags, status per artist, and a progress bar

Artists, roster with status for each step, a final status pill for validation eligibility, and a final stage management validation state

Audit log, time stamped actions with user and role on every create, edit, and sign off

Settings, ownership and permissions, default templates, expiration policy, competency mapping

Steps section, required fields

Step name

Owning department, rigging, coaching, stage management

Prerequisite steps, can choose one or more required steps

Conditions, work lights or show conditions

Validation type, induction, technical induction, full track rehearsal, show validation

Sign off authority, only HOD, AHOD, or Lead from the owning department, must be enforced by permissions

Notes for internal guidance

Expected duration, optional

Attachment upload, optional, for reference photos or quick guides

Artists section, required behavior

Add or remove artists from the program

For each artist, display each step with status, not started, in progress, complete

A Sign Off button appears only when the logged in user has the correct department role and the artist has met prerequisites for that step

Signing off logs a date and time stamp and the exact user and role, and locks that step from edits except by stage management with a reason note

Final stage management validation appears only after all required steps are complete

When stage management validates, store date validated, validator name, and show conditions state

Sign off modal flow

Shows the artist, program, step, prerequisites check, owning department, and the signer’s role

Requires an optional note for context, and always writes a date and time stamp

On confirm, update the step to complete and write a line to the audit log

Data model, high level

Do not create code here, only define how data should be modeled.

Program

id, name, scene, act or cue, color tag, departments involved, created by, created at, updated at

ProgramStep

id, program id, name, owning department, type, induction or technical or rehearsal or show validation, conditions, work lights or show, prerequisites, array of ProgramStep ids, sign off authority, HOD or AHOD or Lead, notes, order

ProgramArtist

id, program id, artist id, status summary, in progress, complete, last activity at

StepStatus

id, program artist id, program step id, status, not started or in progress or complete, signed off by user id, signed off by role, HOD or AHOD or Lead, signed off at, note

FinalValidation

id, program artist id, validated by user id, validated by role, always stage management, validated at, conditions, show, notes

AuditLine

id, entity type and id, user id, role, action, created program, edited step, signed off step, final validation, timestamp, note

Permissions and access control

Only HOD, AHOD, and Lead from the owning department can sign off their department steps

Coaching can sign off choreography and movement steps only, they do not sign technical approvals

Rigging signs off technical inductions, harness fit, cable, trolley, track walk

Stage management signs off any neutral step defined as show level oversight and always performs the final show validation

Only stage management can revert a sign off, requires a note and is always logged

Progress logic and gating

A step can be started only if all prerequisites are complete

Sign off button should be disabled until prerequisites are met and the logged in user has the correct role

Final stage management validation is available only when all required steps are complete

When a program is fully complete for an artist, emit a system event that the artist is eligible for competency assignment in this position

Show a clear badge, Eligible for competency, once the final validation is done

Competency link and expiration signals

This page does not issue competencies, but it must surface competency status and warnings.

Store date validated and last time performed

Compute days since last performance

If no activity for 90 days, mark the related competency as expired and require revalidation

Show a warning seven days before the ninety day mark to rigging, coaching, and stage management for planning

Integration points, read and write

Do not write code, only define the events.

On final stage management validation, emit ProgramCompleted for artist id and program id

On every sign off, emit ProgramStepCompleted with artist id, program step id, department, and signer role

Provide a read endpoint to the lineup and competency modules to query an artist current program status and eligibility

When a performer is restricted by PWD, Training Programs should still allow viewing and planning, but sign off and final validation must be disabled and the UI must show Restricted by PWD

UX details

Mobile friendly first, big tap targets for sign off

Clear progress bars at the program and artist levels

Color tags appear on the program header and in the list view, green for Act, yellow for Cue, orange for Acrobatic Cue

Use simple language labels, avoid jargon, include tooltips for any specialized term

Fast scan design, list row shows progress percent, next step, and last activity time

Templates

Provide a starter set of reusable templates for common flows based on the transcript.

Harness and trolley induction

Inverted familiarization plus track walk

Dual track full rehearsal P1 and P2

Show conditions validation
Allow cloning a template into a new program, then allow edits without changing the original template.

Manual override rules

Out of scope for complex logic, but support a minimal flow for low risk roles like background citizens.

A single checkbox, Manual override for simple substitution, visible to stage management

Requires a short note and writes to audit

Does not mark the program complete and does not award a competency

Creates a traceable record and shows a temporary badge for the day

Migration readiness

Include a hidden tag field for future import mapping from FileMaker

Allow bulk import placeholders for steps and artists, without committing to schema

Acceptance criteria

Only HOD, AHOD, or Lead in the owning department can sign off their steps, confirmed by a role check

Prerequisites block progression and sign off until the required steps are complete

Final stage management validation is required to mark a program complete for an artist

Every sign off writes a date and time stamp and the exact user and role to the audit log

When a program is complete for an artist, the system exposes an Eligible for competency state to the competencies module

Warning banner appears seven days before the ninety day inactivity rule, and the competency changes to expired after ninety days without activity

List and detail views are responsive and usable on mobile

Color coding follows green for Act, yellow for Cue, orange for Acrobatic Cue

Success measures

Time to sign off a step on mobile is under ten seconds

At least ninety percent of sign offs happen inside the app, not through email

Stage management can see a real time roster of who is eligible for validation across programs
